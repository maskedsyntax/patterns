# PRIORITY CHANGES (do these first)

These take precedence over the long-term ERP roadmap below. Move on to the
rest of the roadmap only after these are shipped.

## P1: Rich-text formatting in the journal

Let users emphasise words while writing a journal entry — at minimum **bold**
and *italics*.

- Add formatting controls to the journal editor (toolbar buttons and/or
  keyboard shortcuts).
- Decide on a storage format that round-trips cleanly (e.g. Markdown or a
  lightweight delta) and migrate/display existing plain-text entries without
  data loss.
- Make sure formatted text renders correctly everywhere an entry is shown
  (list previews, detail view, PDF export).
- Verify both rendering paths: `lib/screens/` (desktop) and
  `lib/mobile/screens/` (mobile).

## P2: Bugfix — allow clearing / resetting a date's journal

Today an empty journal cannot be saved, so an entry written on the wrong date
(e.g. meant for the 11th but written on the 12th) cannot be emptied and moved.

- Add an explicit "Reset / clear this date's entry" action with a confirmation
  pop-up before deletion.
- After reset, the date should behave as if it was never written (free to
  re-enter, no leftover empty record blocking re-use).
- Confirm the OCD log path has the same gap and fix it there too if so.

## P3: Gentle, non-strict validation messages

When a required field is missing and saving isn't possible, show clear
validation messages — but keep the tone warm and supportive. This is an app
for people managing OCD and other mental-health struggles, so messages should
be kind and encouraging, never blunt or scolding.

- Audit every save flow (journal, OCD log, etc.) for required-field checks.
- Replace any harsh/technical errors with gentle copy.

## P4: Bugfix — "Rate Patterns" does nothing on Android & iOS

The rate feature is broken on both mobile platforms. It should reliably send
the user somewhere they can rate the app.

- Prefer the native in-app review flow (e.g. `in_app_review`) when available.
- Otherwise fall back to opening the platform store listing
  (Play Store on Android, App Store on iOS).
- See `ReviewPromptService` in `lib/services/` for the existing entry point.

## Product Vision

Most OCD apps function as journals. Users record thoughts, compulsions, and anxiety levels, but receive little guidance on what to do next.

Patterns should move beyond passive tracking and become an active ERP companion that helps users apply evidence-based OCD treatment techniques in their daily lives.

The goal is not simply to document OCD.

The goal is to help users practice recovery.

---

# Core Philosophy

## Passive Tracking

Examples:
- Log intrusive thoughts
- Record compulsions
- Rate anxiety
- View history

Useful for awareness, but often insufficient for meaningful behavioral change.

---

## Active ERP Support

Examples:
- Delay compulsions
- Practice response prevention
- Plan exposures
- Complete ERP exercises
- Build tolerance for uncertainty
- Track behavioral progress

Patterns should prioritize this approach.

---

# Feature 1: Compulsion Delay Tool

## Goal

Help users practice delaying compulsions rather than immediately acting on them.

## Flow

1. User selects a compulsion.
2. User chooses a delay duration.
3. Timer begins.
4. User sits with discomfort.
5. Reflection appears afterward.

## Reflection Questions

- Did the urge decrease?
- Did you resist?
- Did you perform the compulsion?
- How strong is the urge now?

## Benefits

Creates distance between urges and actions while strengthening response prevention.

---

# Feature 2: Guided ERP Exercises

## Goal

Provide structured ERP exercises directly inside the app.

## Example Exercises

### Delay Checking

Resist checking behavior for a chosen duration.

### Delay Reassurance Seeking

Avoid asking for reassurance during the exercise.

### Delay Googling

Postpone researching symptoms or fears.

### Delay Rumination

Notice the urge to mentally solve uncertainty without engaging.

### Delay Washing

Wait before washing or cleaning.

## Exercise Structure

- Introduction
- Why it works
- Instructions
- Timer or activity
- Reflection

---

# Feature 3: ERP Action Planner

## Goal

Plan responses before triggers occur.

## Example

Trigger:
"I feel the urge to Google a symptom."

Response:
"I will wait 15 minutes before searching."

## Fields

- Situation / Trigger
- Planned Action
- Date
- Notes
- Completion Status

---

# Feature 4: Exposure Hierarchy Builder

## Goal

Help users gradually confront fears through ERP.

## Example

Compulsion:
Checking Locks

Exposure Ladder:

1. Leave room without rechecking.
2. Leave house after checking once.
3. Wait 15 minutes before checking.
4. Entire day without checking.
5. Weekend without checking.

## Features

- Difficulty rating
- Anxiety rating
- Progress tracking
- Completion history

---

# Feature 5: Coping Strategy Library

## Goal

Provide healthy alternatives during periods of anxiety.

## Categories

### During Urges

- Deep breathing
- Grounding techniques
- Urge surfing
- Acceptance statements
- Mindful observation

### During Anxiety

- Progressive muscle relaxation
- Mindfulness exercises
- Self-compassion prompts
- Walking exercises

---

# Feature 6: Urge Surfing Tracker

## Goal

Help users experience urges without responding to them.

## Flow

1. Rate urge intensity.
2. Start timer.
3. Re-rate periodically.
4. Visualize urge changes.

## Metrics

- Initial urge
- Peak urge
- Final urge
- Time elapsed

## Benefits

Teaches that urges naturally rise and fall without compulsions.

---

# Feature 7: Response Prevention Tracker

## Goal

Track ERP outcomes after exposures and urges.

## Questions

- Did you resist the compulsion?
- Did you delay it?
- Did you partially perform it?
- Did you fully perform it?
- How anxious were you?

## Metrics

- ERP success rate
- Delay success rate
- Exposure completion rate
- Average urge reduction

---

# Feature 8: Emergency OCD Toolkit

## Goal

Provide immediate support during high-distress moments.

## Quick Actions

- Start Delay Timer
- Start Breathing Exercise
- Begin Urge Surfing
- View Coping Statements
- Review ERP Reminders
- Open Personal Notes

---

# Feature 9: Implementation Intentions

## Goal

Create automatic responses to OCD triggers.

## Examples

If I feel the urge to Google a symptom,
then I will wait 15 minutes first.

If I feel uncertain,
then I will continue what I was doing.

If I feel the urge to ask for reassurance,
then I will write my thoughts down instead.

---

# Feature 10: Structured ERP Programs

## Goal

Guide users through progressively harder ERP exercises.

## Example Program

### Week 1

Delay compulsions for 1 minute.

### Week 2

Delay compulsions for 5 minutes.

### Week 3

Delay compulsions for 15 minutes.

### Week 4

Complete one exposure each day.

---

# Feature 11: Behavioral Experiments

## Goal

Challenge OCD predictions through evidence gathering.

## Example

Prediction:
"If I don't check the lock five times, someone will break in."

Experiment:
Check once and leave.

Result:
Record what actually happened.

## Fields

- Fear Prediction
- Confidence (%)
- Experiment
- Outcome
- Learning

---

# Feature 12: Exposure Reflection Journal

## Goal

Capture insights after ERP exercises.

## Questions

- What happened?
- What did OCD predict?
- What actually happened?
- What did I learn?
- What would I do differently next time?

---

# Feature 13: Uncertainty Training

## Goal

Teach willingness to live with uncertainty.

## Exercises

- Maybe, maybe not statements
- Leaving questions unanswered
- Resisting certainty-seeking
- Probability estimation challenges

---

# Feature 14: ERP Streaks & Recovery Metrics

## Metrics

- Exposures completed
- Delayed compulsions
- Urges surfed
- Response prevention streak
- Weekly ERP consistency

Focus on effort rather than symptom elimination.

---

# Long-Term Product Direction

Patterns should evolve from:

"OCD Journal"

into

"Daily ERP Companion"

The app should help users:

- Understand OCD
- Plan exposures
- Delay compulsions
- Resist reassurance seeking
- Track ERP progress
- Build tolerance for uncertainty
- Practice recovery every day

The primary action inside the app should not be journaling.

The primary action should be doing ERP.
